Property-Based Testing (PBT) is a widely adopted random testing methodology that
combines user-defined logical propositions with automated test case generation.
%
Over the past two decades, PBT has gained significant traction; today, virtually all
major programming languages host multiple competing PBT frameworks, each innovating in
interface design and underlying techniques.
%
As a result of this proliferation, PBT landscape of today is significantly dispersed.
Advances in frameworks have failed to translate to others; tips, tricks, techniques
haven't been quantitatively evaluated, historical artifacts have led to stalled
progress, and our understanding of PBT hasn't shifted to the point it is possible to do
so.

This thesis is a step towards consolidation of the PBT knowledge. It combines a
conceptual framework for expressing property runners in the wild, a flexible property
language for expressing a variety of property runners distributed throughout the PBT
ecosystem, ETNA, the first large scale evaluation and benchmarking platform for PBT,
Marauders, the first declarative hand-crafted mutation management framework, and DIRT,
to address the remaining challenges and mark the future work required for further
consolidation.
